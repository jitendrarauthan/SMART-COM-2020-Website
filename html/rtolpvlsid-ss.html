Call for Papers for Special Track on
“Recent Trends on Low Power VLSI Design”

June 26-27, 2020

Chaired and Organized by

Dr. Sanjay Kumar Singh, Professor, ABESEC Ghaziabad

Email: Sanjaysinghraj4@gmail.com

The main objective of this session is to provide the platform to the researchers, industry experts, academicians and innovative individuals to get together and discuss the “Recent Trends on Low Power VLSI Design”. Technology plays major role in augmenting the human life driven with the advances in hardware capabilities. The platform hence also tries to collate efforts in microelectronics, circuit, optimization and applications including power reduction for effective analysis.



RECOMMENDED TOPICS:



  • Low power input output port design using clock gating technique
  • Battery Simulation-Matching Power Source to Electronic System
  • Minimum energy CMOS design with dual subthreshold supply and multiple logic-level gates
  • An Efficient Algorithm to Selectively Gate Scan Cells for Capture Power Reduction
  • Post Sign-off Leakage Power Optimization
  • Advance VLSI and Embedded System, VLSI Signal processing
  • Robust design of power-efficient VLSI circuits
  • VLSI for Wireless Communication 5G and beyond
  • VLSI for Instrumentation and Control
  • VLSI Design and IC Technology
  • VLSI Architecture of Hybrid Image Compression Model
  • Low-Power Clock Tree Design for Pre-Bond Testing of 3-D Stacked ICs
  • Single Chip Sensor Node Processor with Communication Centric Design
  • Memory system, memory architecture, hardware software codesign


SUBMISSION PROCEDURE:



Researchers and practitioners are invited to submit papers for this special theme session on "Recent Trends on Low Power VLSI Design", on or before 15th May 2020. All submissions must be original and may not be under review by another publication.

INTERESTED AUTHORS CAN CONSULT THE CONFERENCE’S GUIDELINES FOR MANUSCRIPT SUBMISSIONS.
